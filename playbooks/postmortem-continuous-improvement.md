# Postmortem & Continuous Improvement Playbook

## Purpose

Help Technical Program Managers analyze completed incidents, identify root causes, define corrective actions, and improve future reliability.

The TPM AI Agent should focus on learning and prevention, not blame.

---

# Postmortem Principles

The TPM AI Agent must:

- Focus on facts and timeline.
- Separate root causes from symptoms.
- Identify improvement opportunities.
- Assign ownership for corrective actions.
- Promote continuous improvement.

The goal is:

"Improve the system, not blame individuals."

---

# Postmortem Structure

## 1. Incident Summary

Capture:

- What happened?
- When did it happen?
- Which services were affected?
- What was the customer impact?

---

## 2. Incident Timeline

Create a chronological view.

| Time | Event | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|

---

## 3. Impact Analysis

Evaluate:

- Customers affected.
- Business impact.
- SLA impact.
- Operational impact.

---

## 4. Root Cause Analysis

Identify:

- Primary root cause.
- Contributing factors.
- Detection gaps.
- Process gaps.

Avoid:

- Individual blame.
- Unsupported assumptions.

---

## 5. What Went Well

Identify:

- Effective actions.
- Good decisions.
- Strong collaboration.

---

## 6. What Could Improve

Identify:

- Process improvements.
- Technical improvements.
- Communication improvements.

---

## 7. Corrective Actions

Create actionable items.

| Action | Owner | Due Date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|

---

## 8. Prevention Strategy

Define:

- Monitoring improvements.
- Automation opportunities.
- Process changes.
- Training needs.

---

# Expected Output

The TPM AI Agent should generate:

1. Executive Summary.
2. Incident Timeline.
3. Root Cause Analysis.
4. Lessons Learned.
5. Corrective Action Plan.
6. Prevention Recommendations.
