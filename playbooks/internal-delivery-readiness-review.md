# Internal Delivery Readiness Review Playbook

## Purpose

Define the internal alignment process performed after receiving a SOW and before customer kickoff.

The objective is to ensure Sales, Architecture, Delivery, and Technical teams share the same understanding of scope, commitments, assumptions, and execution approach.

---

# Phase 1 — Team Assignment

The TPM AI Agent should identify required roles:

- Technical Lead.
- Solution Architect.
- Engineering Resources.
- Project/Program Manager.
- Security Representatives.
- Operations Representatives.

Consider:

- Required skills.
- Technology experience.
- Availability.
- Previous customer knowledge.

---

# Phase 2 — Internal SOW Review

The technical team reviews:

## Scope

Validate:

- What was sold.
- What will be delivered.
- What is excluded.

---

## Assumptions

Identify:

- Customer responsibilities.
- Technical prerequisites.
- Environmental conditions.

---

## Dependencies

Identify:

- External teams.
- Customer actions.
- Vendor dependencies.

---

## Commitments

Validate:

- Delivery dates.
- Service levels.
- Acceptance criteria.

---

# Phase 3 — Solution Validation

Review:

- Architecture documents.
- Solution design.
- Technical diagrams.
- Bill of materials.
- Licensing requirements.

Validate:

- Design feasibility.
- Missing components.
- Technical risks.

---

# Phase 4 — Open Questions Management

Create:

- Clarification list.
- Owner.
- Due date.
- Impact.

Questions must be resolved before customer kickoff when possible.

---

# Phase 5 — Customer Kickoff Readiness

Before kickoff validate:

## Internal Alignment

Everyone understands:

- Objectives.
- Scope.
- Roles.
- Risks.
- Timeline.

## Customer Message

The team agrees on:

- What was committed.
- What is not included.
- Required customer actions.

---

# TPM Principle

Never start customer execution with internal ambiguity.

Internal alignment creates external confidence.
