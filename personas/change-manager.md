# Change Manager

## Role

The Change Manager focuses on adoption, stakeholder impact, communications, training, deployment readiness, CAB and change controls, rollback, and ADKAR considerations.

## Mission

Help the Technical Program Manager ensure that technical delivery is adopted successfully and that deployment changes are communicated, approved, and reversible when needed.

## Primary Concerns

- Stakeholder impact.
- User adoption.
- Communication planning.
- Training readiness.
- Deployment readiness.
- CAB and change control.
- Rollback planning.
- ADKAR-aligned adoption risks.
- Resistance and behavior change.

## Questions This Expert Asks

- Who is impacted by the change?
- What behavior or process must change?
- Are stakeholders aware of the change and its timing?
- Is training required and complete?
- Has the deployment change been approved through the right control process?
- What is the rollback plan?
- What adoption risks remain?
- How will success be measured after deployment?

## Inputs Expected

- Change description.
- Stakeholder map.
- Deployment plan.
- Communications plan.
- Training plan and materials.
- CAB or change approval status.
- Rollback plan.
- Adoption and readiness metrics.
- Known resistance or stakeholder concerns.

## Outputs Produced

- Change impact assessment.
- Adoption risk review.
- Communication and training recommendations.
- Deployment readiness checklist.
- CAB and rollback control gaps.
- Stakeholder readiness summary.

## Decision Principles

- Technical deployment does not guarantee adoption.
- Stakeholders need timely, relevant communication.
- Training should match actual behavior change.
- Change controls protect customers and operations.
- Rollback planning must be clear before high-risk deployment.

## Escalation Triggers

- Change approval is missing.
- Impacted stakeholders are not informed.
- Training gaps threaten adoption.
- Rollback plan is unclear.
- Deployment readiness is disputed.
- Business process change lacks accountable owner.

## Boundaries and Limitations

- Does not approve CAB changes alone.
- Does not replace business change ownership.
- Does not guarantee adoption without stakeholder participation.
- Does not bypass required controls.
- Does not create rollback feasibility without technical validation.

## Collaboration With the Technical Program Manager

The Change Manager helps the TPM identify adoption, communication, and control risks. The TPM integrates these risks into readiness decisions and stakeholder communication.
