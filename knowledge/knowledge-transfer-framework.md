# Knowledge Transfer Framework

## Purpose

Define how the TPM AI Agent evaluates, plans, executes, and validates Knowledge Transfer (KT) activities during Service Transition.

The objective is to ensure that operational teams acquire the knowledge, confidence, and evidence required to independently support the delivered solution.

Knowledge Transfer is not measured by the completion of training sessions.

Knowledge Transfer is measured by operational capability.

---

# KT Principles

The TPM AI Agent should ensure that Operations can:

- Explain the solution.
- Operate the solution.
- Troubleshoot common failures.
- Escalate correctly.
- Recover the service.
- Perform routine maintenance.

---

# KT Readiness Levels

## Level 1 — Awareness

Operations understands:

- Business purpose.
- High-level architecture.
- Major components.

---

## Level 2 — Operational Knowledge

Operations understands:

- Daily activities.
- Monitoring.
- Alerts.
- Incident process.
- Change process.

---

## Level 3 — Technical Ownership

Operations can independently:

- Diagnose failures.
- Execute recovery procedures.
- Perform maintenance.
- Coordinate vendor support.

# KT Validation

The TPM AI Agent should recommend validating KT through evidence.

Examples:

- Hands-on exercises.
- Shadow support sessions.
- Reverse demonstrations.
- Operational simulations.
- Runbook execution.

Passive attendance is not sufficient evidence of readiness.

---

# KT Deliverables

The TPM AI Agent should verify the existence of:

- Training Material
- Operational Runbooks
- Troubleshooting Guides
- FAQ
- Architecture Documentation
- Recorded Sessions (if applicable)

---

# KT Success Criteria

Knowledge Transfer is successful when Operations demonstrates the ability to support the service without assistance from the project team.

---

# TPM Principle

Training people is not the objective.

Creating operational confidence is.
