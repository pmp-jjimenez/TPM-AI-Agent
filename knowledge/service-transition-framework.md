# Service Transition Framework

## Purpose

Define how the TPM AI Agent prepares a technology solution for successful transition from project delivery to operational ownership.

The objective is to ensure that Operations can confidently support, maintain, and evolve the delivered solution.

Project completion alone does not indicate operational readiness.

---

# Transition Principle

A project is considered complete only when:

- The customer accepts the deliverables.
- Operations accepts ownership.
- Support teams can independently operate the service.

---

# Transition Objectives

The TPM AI Agent should ensure:

- Complete documentation.
- Knowledge transfer.
- Monitoring readiness.
- Support readiness.
- Operational ownership.
- Service continuity.

---

# Transition Domains

## Documentation

Verify availability of:

- High-Level Design (HLD)
- Low-Level Design (LLD)
- As-Built Documentation
- Network Diagrams
- Architecture Diagrams
- Configuration Guides
- Installation Guides
- Operational Runbooks
- Troubleshooting Guides

---

## Operational Readiness

Validate:

- Monitoring configured.
- Alerting configured.
- Backups validated.
- Disaster Recovery documented.
- Capacity baselined.
- Performance validated.

---

## Support Readiness

Confirm:

- L1 readiness.
- L2 readiness.
- L3 ownership.
- Escalation paths.
- On-call responsibilities.

---

## Knowledge Transfer

Verify that support teams understand:

- Architecture.
- Dependencies.
- Recovery procedures.
- Known Issues.
- Maintenance activities.

---

## Service Ownership

Confirm ownership for:

- Infrastructure.
- Applications.
- Security.
- Monitoring.
- Incident Response.
- Change Management.

---

# Transition Gates

The TPM AI Agent should evaluate:

Gate 1

Documentation Complete

Gate 2

Operations Ready

Gate 3

Knowledge Transfer Completed

Gate 4

Monitoring Operational

Gate 5

Support Ownership Accepted

---

# Readiness Principle

Operational readiness is measured by evidence, not by assumption.
