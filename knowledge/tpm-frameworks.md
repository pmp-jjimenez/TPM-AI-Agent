# TPM Frameworks Knowledge Base

## Purpose

Provide the TPM AI Agent with reusable frameworks and professional practices for managing complex technology programs.

The agent should select the appropriate framework depending on project context, complexity, risk, and organizational maturity.

The TPM AI Agent should avoid applying frameworks by default. 
It should first evaluate organizational complexity, delivery model, team structure, and business constraints.

---

# Program Delivery Models

## Predictive Delivery

Use when:

- Scope is stable.
- Requirements are well defined.
- Regulatory compliance is important.
- Changes are controlled.

Examples:

- Infrastructure deployments.
- Data center migrations.
- Enterprise system implementations.

---

## Agile Delivery

Use when:

- Requirements evolve.
- Customer feedback is continuous.
- Teams deliver incrementally.

Examples:

- Software development.
- Product enhancements.

---

## Hybrid Delivery

Use when:

- Enterprise programs require governance.
- Development teams work iteratively.
- Multiple delivery approaches coexist.

Examples:

- Cloud migrations.
- Digital transformations.
- Enterprise platforms.

---

## Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe)

Use when:

- Multiple Agile teams must coordinate delivery.
- The organization manages large technology programs.
- Dependencies across teams create delivery complexity.
- Business and technology planning must be synchronized.

SAFe provides structure for scaling Agile practices across an enterprise.

---

# SAFe Concepts Relevant for TPMs

## Agile Release Train (ART)

A virtual organization of multiple teams working toward a shared business objective.

The TPM AI Agent should understand:

- ART objectives.
- Team dependencies.
- Release coordination.

---

## Program Increment (PI) Planning

A planning event where teams align:

- Objectives.
- Dependencies.
- Risks.
- Delivery commitments.

TPM focus:

- Cross-team alignment.
- Dependency management.
- Risk visibility.

---

## Roles and Collaboration

Important roles:

- Release Train Engineer (RTE)
- Product Management
- Product Owners
- System Architects
- Development Teams

The TPM AI Agent should help coordinate across these roles.

---

## SAFe Governance Considerations

For SAFe environments, the agent should focus on:

- Dependency tracking.
- PI objectives.
- Incremental delivery.
- Program risks.
- Stakeholder alignment.

---

## When Not to Use SAFe

Avoid recommending SAFe when:

- The initiative is small.
- A single team can deliver independently.
- The organization does not have Agile maturity.
- Additional governance would create unnecessary overhead.

# RAID Management

The TPM AI Agent should maintain visibility into:

## Risks

Potential future events that may impact objectives.

Evaluate:

- Probability.
- Impact.
- Mitigation.
- Owner.

---

## Assumptions

Conditions believed to be true for planning purposes.

Validate assumptions regularly.

---

## Issues

Current problems requiring action.

Track:

- Impact.
- Owner.
- Resolution plan.

---

## Dependencies

External factors required for success.

Track:

- Dependency owner.
- Required date.
- Impact if delayed.

---

# Stakeholder Management

The TPM AI Agent should analyze stakeholders by:

- Influence.
- Impact.
- Decision authority.
- Communication needs.

Executive stakeholders require:

- Business impact.
- Decisions required.
- Risks.
- Recommendations.

Technical teams require:

- Context.
- Requirements.
- Dependencies.
- Actions.

# Program Governance

A mature program should define:

## Executive Governance

Purpose:

- Strategic alignment.
- Decisions.
- Escalations.

Example:

Steering Committee.

---

## Delivery Governance

Purpose:

- Execution tracking.
- Risks.
- Dependencies.

Example:

Weekly program review.

---

## Operational Governance

Purpose:

- Service stability.
- Continuous improvement.

Example:

Operational reviews.

# TPM Principle

The TPM AI Agent should always connect:

Technology → Business Impact → Decision → Action
