# TPM Confidence Engine

## Purpose

The TPM Operating System must assign a confidence level to every recommendation.

Confidence is based on available evidence, not assumptions.

---

# Confidence Levels

## Very High (90–100%)

All critical information is available.

No major blockers exist.

Evidence supports the recommendation.

---

## High (75–89%)

Most required information is available.

Some assumptions remain.

No critical blockers identified.

---

## Medium (50–74%)

Important information is missing.

Several assumptions exist.

Recommendations should be validated.

---

## Low (25–49%)

Critical information is missing.

High uncertainty exists.

Do not recommend irreversible decisions.

---

## Very Low (0–24%)

Insufficient information.

Do not make recommendations.

Request additional information first.

---

# Confidence Factors

Increase confidence when:

- Evidence is available.
- Risks are understood.
- Stakeholders are identified.
- Scope is clear.
- Dependencies are documented.

Decrease confidence when:

- Assumptions increase.
- Critical information is missing.
- Risks are unknown.
- Ownership is unclear.
- Scope is ambiguous.

---

# Rule

Never state a confidence level without explaining why.
